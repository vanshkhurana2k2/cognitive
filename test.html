Our Test

Our Neurocognitive Screening test uses various question-types to better evaluate the 5 skills that comprise cognition.

  • Comprehension

    Determines an individual’s ability to understand and respond to instructions. For example, this could be to spell words forwards and backwards. If someone spells a word forwards when instructions clearly state to spell backwards, the person may have been unable to comprehend the task’s directions.

  • Perception

    Evaluates the individual’s ability to identify and distinguish between sensory information. Our test mainly evaluates visual and auditory perception, testing users with the help of images and recordings. If an individual was unable to identify an image or recording, he or she may have an impaired perception.

  • Motor Skills

    Assess the user’s ability to physically respond to cues, along with the time associated with completing the task. For example, a user may be asked to press a button. If unable to press the button in a certain time period, the individual’s motor skills may be impaired.

  • Memory

    Determines the user’s ability to retain and recall information that may be presented in a variety of formats. A person who cannot recall information may display memory impairment, a common symptom of many neurodegenerative diseases.

  • Problem Solving

    Assesses the user’s ability to answer basic questions involving numerical/language skills. These questions also require elements of comprehension. A user who is unable to correctly answer a question may possess poor problem-solving skills.
